Q: What are pilsners, ales, lagers, stouts etc? What does "bottom fermented mean?" When you say "lagered" What in the heck does that mean? A: "Beer" refers to any fermented beverage made from grain. Lagers and ales are two families of beer, distinguished by the type of yeast and the temperature of the fermentation process. Lagers are fermented at a cooler temperature. " Bottom Fermenting Yeasr" in a lager is cooled for a number of weeks before the brew is ready to be enjoyed. Ales are fermented in warmer conditions known as "top fermenting yeast". Thus, Ales are ready to drink more quickly than their slower cousins, the lager. There are many distinct styles of beer within the lager and ale families: for example, pilsner is one of the most popular lager styles; and porter and stout are examples of ale styles. And in both families, beers can run the gamut from light to dark-colored, and from weak to strong alcohol.

Q: What is the proper way to pour a beer? A: If you pour the beer slowly down the side of a tilted glass, a smaller head is formed and more CO2 remains dissolved in the beer. If you hold a glass upright and pour straight down you will generate a larger head. Ales typically have about 2 fat fingers worth of head. Lagers have a little larger head that pillows atop the glass.
